About

1.6 MeV Proton Injector

I am actively developing the control system for Bridge12's new 1.6 MeV Proton Injector, including the firmware for our various embedded subsystems as well as the technician's graphical control software. Pictured above is a test chamber for our vacuum control subsystem. Injector systems like these are crucial components in cutting-edge proton therapy devices used for cancer treatment. The first of these systems incorporating our injectors will go on to treat patients in Mass General Hospital.



Gyrotrons

During my time at Bridge12 I have worked on the control systems for a variety of gyrotrons in a wide range of frequencies and power. More recently I have worked towards modularizing the design with a universally adaptable control software that considerably streamlines our controls development. At the hardware level, these systems are fast, reliable, and fail-safe through the utilization of embedded controllers. At the software level, controls are designed to be intuitive, powerful, and dare I say beautiful.



Frequency Monitoring System (FMS)

The Bridge12 FMS is a precision measurement system used to track the output frequency of terahertz sources, such as traveling wave tubes or gyrotrons. Frequencies that high are beyond the scope of a typical frequency counter and existing solutions are as scarce as they are expensive. Our device is able to measure and track signals up to 1 THz and features remote control from a tablet or desktop application. Moreover, this compact system replaces a rack full of expensive instrumentation with a turn-key device the size of a shoe box. Blood Transport Device

For a design project in sustainable engineering, I worked with a small team to design a device meant for transporting blood. The goal was to keep a liter sample between 4-6°C for as long as possible without access to grid power. The chosen design was based around a custom lid for a vacuum flask that would thermoelectrically cool or warm the interior. Packs of phase-change material with a 5°C melting pointer are also kept in the flask to stabilize the temperature of the sample. Our prototype was able to hold within range for over three full, very cold days before succumbing to electrical failure in the elements.


Vacuum Bakeout System

Bridge12 has an in-house exhausting station which is used to remove volatile compounds from vacuum devices via the process colloquially known as a bakeout. I had the privilege of developing the control system for the recent system redesign. The previous system required lots of manual operation and depended on clunky LabView software, whereas the new system automates the majority of the bakeout process and features a user-friendly, efficient control software written in C++. The low level control is also far more robust in this revision through the utilization of our multipurpose signal interface board, for which I wrote the firmware.


A New Kind of Musical Instrument

In the fall of 2019 I sought to create a new kind of musical instrument for an open-ended design project in one of my courses. The idea was a handheld digital synthesizer which modulates its pitch according to alitude. The design was intended to be as affordable as possible without sacrificing the minimum power necessary for proper sythesis. The functional prototype came out under $60 and is able to generate and modulate three different waveforms that is plays through a built-in speaker. The device also features a rechargeable battery, bluetooth connectivity, and drag-and-drop sample uploads.
