Bridge12 Frequency Monitoring System


I am currently employed by Bridge12 Technologies, Inc. located in Framingham, MA. Over the summer of 2017 I was responsible for the development of a new control system for the high frequency signal monitoring system, written primarily in C++. I am responsible for the user interface and controls, as well as the application of wave motion physics in the signal processing algorithms. In short, the device aims to measure and track frequencies which are too high for a typical frequency counter. The device mixes a user-input signal with a local signal of known frequency and performs some math on the output to determine the source frequency. You can read more about the system on our website.

Electron Spin Resonance

For the purpose of better understanding electronic spin resonance, I investigated the organic chemical compound 2,2-diphenyl-1-picrylhydrazyl (DPPH) within a basic RF unit. Using data visualized on an oscilloscope I was able to identify and measure the width of resonance for a given input frequency. Using that information in addition to voltage measurements from the ammeter I calculated an expected magnitude of the magnetic field within the Helmholtz coils. If you would like to read more you can find my report here.


Atomic Force Microscopy

For the purpose of better understanding the mechanisms, capabilities, and shortcomings of atomic force microscopy, I investigated the high resolution scans of an atomic force microscope.
