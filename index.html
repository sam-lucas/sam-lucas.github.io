About

Hi, my name is Sam. I'm an engineer and entrepreneur with a fierce passion for building and learning. My specialty lies in designing and developing large-scale cloud-based system, and B2B software product development.

Notable past projects of mine include proton accelerator control systems used in cancer treatment, autonomous COVID-detection kiosks, and an AI-powered rules-based anomaly detection cloud platform used in high-risk environments.

CypherMed Cloud

My current focus is developing the software product and business known as CypherMed Cloud, which is a platform design for medical devices. Originally conceived as an internal tool at Promenade Software, I worked with the President to establish a subsidary business focused on developing and marking this platform as a off-the-shelf B2B solution. I currently operate as Chief Technology Officer.


Kolosos

During my time at Loko AI, I worked on Kolosos (formerly Caretaker), a real-time computer vision platform that takes any video input and instantly provides actionable insights and IoT automation for risk management, security, safety and defense. Kolosos was designed to be a highly scalable and performant machine learning tool. Incorporated in the efficient microservice design is my custom stream engine, which allows for secure, ultra-low latency processing of video streams. Variants of the system were also developed for GovCloud and local stacks so as to better serve our military customers and others with stringent security requirements.


Autonomous Temperature Detector (ATD)

The ATD is a hands-free infrared thermometer in a kiosk form factor that automatically measures human body temperature at the inner-eye canthi for rapid elevated temperature screening. I was tasked with developing the control firmware and accompanying cloud service for this product. A facial recognition algorithm locks onto a user within proximity and captures their temperature from a high resolution thermal image, within a tolerance of ±0.2°C in less than a second.


1.6 MeV Proton Injector

During my time at Bridge12, I developed the control system for their latest 1.6 MeV Proton Injector, including the firmware for various embedded subsystems as well as the technician's graphical control software (pictured above). Injector systems like these are crucial components in cutting-edge proton therapy devices used for cancer treatment. The first of these systems incorporating Bridge12 injectors will go on to treat patients in Mass General Hospital.



Vacuum Bakeout System

Bridge12 has an in-house exhausting station which is used to remove volatile compounds from vacuum devices via the process colloquially known as a bakeout. I had the privilege of developing the control system for the recent system redesign. The previous system required lots of manual operation and depended on clunky LabView software, whereas the new system automates the majority of the bakeout process and features a user-friendly, efficient control software. The low level control is also far more robust in this revision through the utilization of our multipurpose signal interface board, for which I wrote the firmware.


Gyrotrons

During my time at Bridge12 I have worked on the control systems for a variety of gyrotrons in a wide range of frequencies and power. More recently I have worked towards modularizing the design with a universally adaptable control software that considerably streamlines our controls development. At the hardware level, these systems are fast, reliable, and fail-safe through the utilization of embedded controllers. At the software level, controls are designed to be intuitive, powerful, and dare I say beautiful.
